Biography

José Pedro Balmaceda Pascal (born 2 April 1975) is a Chilean-American actor. After nearly two decades of taking small roles in film and television, Pascal rose to prominence for portraying Oberyn Martell during the fourth season of the HBO fantasy series Game of Thrones (2014) and Javier Peña in the Netflix crime series Narcos (2015–2017). Since 2019, he has starred as the title character in the Disney+ Star Wars series The Mandalorian and since 2023, he has played Joel in the HBO drama series The Last of Us. He also reprised his Mandalorian role in the spin-off series The Book of Boba Fett (2022). Outside of television, Pascal has appeared in the films The Adjustment Bureau (2011), The Great Wall (2016), Kingsman: The Golden Circle (2017), The Equalizer 2 (2018), Triple Frontier (2019), Wonder Woman 1984 (2020), and The Unbearable Weight of Massive Talent (2022).Click here to read more

Personal Life

Pascal is fluent in both English and Spanish. He has lived in New York City since 1993.[10] He is close friends with actress Sarah Paulson, having met her soon after moving to New York.[37]Pascal is an advocate of LGBTQ+ rights.[38] He was supportive of his sister Lux Pascal when she came out as transgender, about which she said, "He has been an important part of this. He is also an artist and has been a guide. He was one of the first to give me the things that formed my identity."[39]Pascal has referred to himself as an agnostic[40] and a liberal.[41] He endorsed Gabriel Boric in the 2021 Chilean presidential election.
